About Temperature Scales

Temperature scales measure thermal energy. Different scales are used in different contexts, from daily weather reports to scientific research.

  • Celsius: Most common worldwide scale
  • Fahrenheit: Used primarily in the USA
  • Kelvin: Absolute scale used in science
  • Different countries use different scales
Conversion Formulas

The conversion between temperature scales follows specific mathematical formulas:

  • °C to °F: (°C × 9/5) + 32
  • °F to °C: (°F - 32) × 5/9
  • °C to K: °C + 273.15
  • K to °C: K - 273.15
  • °F to K: (°F - 32) × 5/9 + 273.15
Practical Applications

Temperature conversion is essential in various fields and daily life situations:

  • Weather forecasting and reporting
  • Scientific experiments and research
  • Cooking and baking recipes
  • Industrial processes and manufacturing
  • Medical temperature measurements
